---
title: "on prioritizing dreams"
date: 2026-04-23
tags: ["life"]
---

The worst period of recovering from (mundane) illness is when you're healthy enough to stay awake and contemplate your life but not healthy enough to exercise away the existential angst.

As I sat in that period yesterday (though I'm partially still in it now), something very obvious dawned on me. I've found that very obvious things dawn on me frustratingly frequently in my 20s. Yesterday, I realized that, if I have a dream of living some sort of life -- a new job, a grand adventure, etc. -- I at some point have to make motions towards that dream, or acknowledge to myself that it will never happen. Like I said, obvious.

I've always known this, but the stretch of life felt a lot longer a few years ago than it does today.

Let me be clear: I don't believe I'm "running out of time." I'm 27 and have many more years ahead of me (god/universe willing). I generally dislike excuses that are based on not "having time." We always have time for whatever we prioritize; when we say we don't have time for something, we're really saying it's not that important to us. But, I digress.

I'm not running out of time, but I am getting rapidly worse at taking big risks and making big changes in my life. Risk is a muscle and, like any muscle, grows cold without use. I could argue that, in my case, the cold muscle is a good thing! Between 22 and 25, I upended my life constantly, moving back and forth across the country, switching to wildly different jobs, and bumming around various places in the Americas. I felt restless and unhappy in all those circumstances. Now, in San Francisco at 27, it's the first time I've really felt content where I am.

But contentedness is a double-edged sword, right? It keeps me happy in the day-to-day, but makes the thought of upending my life to pursue a macro-level dream much harder. Do I really want to risk my well-paying, stable, flexible job for a career more like the one I dreamed about, one more in line with my values? Or chase a grand adventure like cycling from Alaska to Argentina? What about my nice apartment in my favorite neighborhood, near all my friends with whom I ride bikes and play spikeball and eat food in the park? What if, after I shoot for my dream, I can't get those things back?
